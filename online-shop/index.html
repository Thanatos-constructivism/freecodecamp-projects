Discover Courses That Inspire

Introduction to Western Philosophy

Explore the foundations of philosophical thought from ancient Greece to the Enlightenment. Discover thinkers like Socrates, Plato, Aristotle, Descartes, and Kant. Perfect for beginners seeking to understand life's big questions.

Philosophy of Art and Aesthetics

Examine what makes something "art," beauty, taste, and the role of art in society. From Plato's views on imitation to modern theories by Kant, Nietzsche, and contemporary thinkers. Ideal for those interested in the intersection of philosophy and creativity.

History of Modern and Contemporary Art

Trace the evolution of art from Impressionism to today's digital and conceptual works. Study key movements like Cubism, Surrealism, Abstract Expressionism, and Pop Art, with analysis of iconic artists and their ideas.

Ethics in Everyday Life

Dive into moral philosophy: virtue ethics, utilitarianism, deontology, and existentialism. Apply ethical frameworks to real-world dilemmas in personal relationships, work, technology, and society. Beginner-friendly with practical discussions.

Renaissance Art and Humanism

Discover how Renaissance masters like Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, and Raphael blended philosophy, science, and art. Explore humanism's impact on painting, sculpture, and architecture during one of history's greatest creative explosions.

Philosophy Through Film and Literature

Analyze deep philosophical themes via movies and novels: existentialism in films like "The Matrix" or "Blade Runner," absurdity in Camus-inspired stories, and identity in modern literature. Engaging and accessible way to explore complex ideas.
